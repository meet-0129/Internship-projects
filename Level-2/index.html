Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The People's President & Missile Man of India

Life Journey of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1954 - Graduated from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli
  • 1960 - Joined DRDO as a scientist
  • 1969 - Transferred to ISRO as project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle
  • 1982 - Became Director of DRDO
  • 1992 - Appointed as Scientific Adviser to the Defence Minister
  • 1998 - Led the Pokhran-II nuclear tests
  • 2002 - Elected as the 11th President of India
  • 2015 - Passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

-- Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Major Achievements

  • Led the development of India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle
  • Played a crucial role in the development of ballistic missile technology
  • Received the Padma Bhushan (1981), Padma Vibhushan (1990), and Bharat Ratna (1997)
  • Authored several books including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds"
  • Known for his vision of making India a developed nation by 2020

Legacy

Dr. Kalam's legacy continues to inspire millions of young Indians. His dedication to education, science, and technology, combined with his humble nature and vision for India's development, makes him one of the most beloved leaders in Indian history.
